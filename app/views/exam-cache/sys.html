Thank you for applying to the Research and Development Unit. At R&D, Excellence is our Habit.Our graduates have landed jobs in companies such as Smart, Oracle, Hewlett-Packard, Teradata, and Misys, among others. We hope that you become our future members.

As a (hopefully) future member of the Systems Division, you are expected to have the following competencies:

  • A mind that is very open to learning
  • Competency in any programming language of your choice
  • The ability to work individually, without the need for direction, and within a team

We are also pushing for these competencies:

  • Writing clean and concise code
  • Involvement in projects both inside and outside the university
  • Knowledge of a software development framework

Good luck!

Programming Knowledge

Write a program that prints the numbers from1 to 100. But for multiples of three print "Fizz" instead of the number and for multiples of five print "Buzz". For numbers which are multiples of both three and five print "FizzBuzz".

You may use any programming language you prefer.

Programming Proficiency

Create an account at Codingbat http://codingbat.com/ and solve as many problems as you can in Java. You will get full credit if you can solve three Array-2 problems, three String-2 problems, one
Array-3 problem, and one String-3 problem.

You may solve less or more problems if you prefer. Less problems mean partial credit, while the more problems you solve, the higher chance you have of being accepted. We have to check your solutions first, so even if you have submitted your screenshots, we will still check your Java source code for conciseness and proper programming technique.

Self-Study Capacity

This is the most important part of the test. As an R&D member, you will be asked to write code that is beyond the scope of your lessons in the university. Hence it is of utmost importance that you be self-directed and study on your own. We are expecting great things from our future members.

Do one of the following:

  • Sign up for an account at (http://www.codecademy.com/learn) and finish three lessons in the following courses: Web Fundamentals, JavaScript, Ruby, or PHP. You will need to learn at least one of these in the future, so you might as well learn it now. Solve three Array-2 problems, three String-2 problems, one Array-3 problem, and one String-3 problem at Codingbat, but use Python. These have to be different from the ones that you solved using Java.

  • Once you have finished, send us a screenshot at systems@uernd.org
